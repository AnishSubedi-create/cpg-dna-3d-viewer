CpG-A (ODN2216) · 20-mer
5'-ggGGGACGATCGTCgggggg-3' · Mixed PS/PO backbone. Four strands tetramerize via poly-G G-quadruplex caps (parallel topology, K+ stabilized at ~2.8 A from O6, PDB 1KF1). Palindromic PO center forms duplex stems. Forms ~100-200 nm globular aggregates. Binds HMGB1 to nucleate LLPS condensates, activating RAGE, SLP76, and inflammasome assembly driving IFN-alpha/beta in pDCs.
Verthelyi et al., J Immunol 2001 · Parkinson et al., Nature 2002 (1KF1) · PNAS 2025

CpG-B (ODN2006) · 24-mer

Ad5 dsDNA · ~36 kbp genome (50 bp segment shown)
B-form dsDNA, 10.5 bp/turn, PO backbone. Protein VII (pVII): 174 aa arginine-rich histone-like protein coating genome at ~1 pVII/20 bp. Compacts viral DNA and sequesters HMGB1 via A-box contact, suppressing IFN-beta. ITRs (~103 bp, AT-rich) at termini. Cytosolic dsDNA escaping post-lysis drives cGAS-STING activation.
Haruki et al., J Virol 2006 · Avgousti et al., Nature 2016

HMGB1 + CpG-B: weak / no stable complex

HMGB1 + Ad37 DNA: pVII sequestration (GD1a tropism)
Identical docking pose to Ad5: pVII conserved across serotypes. HMGB1 A-box minor groove insertion + pVII major groove binding. LLPS role: Downstream sensor for condensates

Key insight: CpG-A drives HMGB1 phase separation because its G-quadruplex tetramer provides multivalent scaffolding. Linear CpG-B cannot replicate this. Adenoviral pVII pre-empts HMGB1-mediated innate sensing by sequestering HMGB1 on viral DNA in the nucleus. HMGB1 redox state then determines whether RAGE or TLR4 is engaged downstream.
