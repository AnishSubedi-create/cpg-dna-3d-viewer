RAGE (AGER) · 35 kDa transmembrane receptor · PDB reference: 4LP5, 3O3U
Three extracellular Ig-like domains: V-domain (residues 23 to 116, principal ligand-binding site with positively charged surface), C1-domain (124 to 221), and C2-domain (227 to 317), followed by transmembrane helix and cytoplasmic tail. V-domain harbors an 8-stranded beta-sandwich; Cys38-Cys99 disulfide stabilizes it. Zn2+ coordinates the C1-C1 dimerization contact (Arg178-Glu168 salt bridge). Constitutive homodimer recruited into LLPS droplets by HMGB1-CpG-A, then recruits DIAPH1 to activate NF-kB and IRF7 signaling.

TLR4 with MD-2 coreceptor · LPS-activated symmetric dimer · PDB 3FXI
TLR4 ectodomain is a 22-repeat LRR horseshoe divided into three subdomains: N-terminal (LRR 1 to 6), central or hypervariable (LRR 7 to 12), and C-terminal (LRR 13 to 22, with ~28% larger beta-sheet radius). MD-2 is a beta-cup fold coreceptor docking to the concave N-terminal surface. Five of six LPS acyl chains bury into the MD-2 hydrophobic pocket; the sixth bridges to conserved Phe residues on TLR4, driving the m-shaped 2:2:2 dimer. HMGB1 contacts the TLR4 N-terminal A-patch but does not promote dimerization, placing it in a distinct signaling mode from LPS.

TLR9 · CpG-DNA activated symmetric dimer · PDB 3WPC
TLR9 forms a symmetric 2:2 complex with CpG-DNA. The ~26-repeat LRR horseshoe carries a Z-loop insertion between LRR 14 and 15 unique to nucleic acid-sensing TLRs. Each CpG-DNA strand is contacted by both protomers: the N-terminal fragment (LRRNT to LRR10) from protomer A and the C-terminal fragment (LRR20 to LRR22) from protomer B. This cross-protomer recognition is why simple CpG monomers must dimerize for full activation. The two bound CpG-ODNs are spaced ~7 nm apart in the active complex. Dimerization juxtaposes TIR domains, recruiting MyD88 and driving IRF7-dependent IFN-alpha/beta in pDCs.

CpG-A · ODN2216

Structure: G-quadruplex tetramer

Backbone: Mixed PS/PO

Size: 20-mer, ~100 to 200 nm aggregate

Sensor: TLR9 (pDC endosome)

HMGB1: Direct binding, LLPS condensates, RAGE activation

LLPS type: HMGB1-CpG-A condensates (RAGE axis)

Output: IFN-alpha/beta, IL-1beta

CpG-B · ODN2006

Structure: Linear ssDNA

Backbone: Full phosphorothioate

Size: 24-mer, ~7 to 8 nm

Sensor: TLR9 (B cells, monocytes)

HMGB1: Weak interaction, no LLPS, direct TLR9 dominant

LLPS type: None

Output: IL-6, TNF-alpha, B-cell activation

Adenoviral dsDNA

Structure: B-form dsDNA, linear

Backbone: Phosphodiester (natural)

Size: ~36 kbp genome

Sensor: cGAS (cytosol), TLR9 weak

HMGB1: Protein VII sequesters via A-box, blocks IFN-beta

LLPS type: cGAS-dsDNA condensates driving STING

Output: IFN-beta, ISG15, MX2

RAGE

Structure: V-C1-C2 Ig-domain homodimer

Ligands: HMGB1, CpG-A DNA, S100 proteins, AGEs

Binding site: V-domain positively charged dimer interface

Dimerization: Constitutive, enhanced by HMGB1-LLPS condensates

Signaling: NF-kB, ERK1/2 through DIAPH1 cytoplasmic recruitment

Role in LLPS: Downstream receptor for HMGB1 condensates

TLR4

Structure: 22-repeat LRR horseshoe with MD-2 coreceptor

Ligands: LPS, HMGB1 (non-dimerizing), lipoteichoic acid

Binding site: MD-2 hydrophobic pocket for LPS, N-terminal A-patch for HMGB1

Dimerization: LPS-induced m-shaped 2:2:2 complex

Signaling: MyD88 and TRIF, NF-kB and IRF3

Role in LLPS: Surface receptor, not a known LLPS participant

TLR9

Structure: ~26-repeat LRR horseshoe, Z-loop at LRR 14-15

Ligands: CpG-A (via HMGB1 and RAGE), CpG-B (direct 1:1)

Binding site: Ascending lateral face, cross-protomer contact with each DNA

Dimerization: CpG-DNA-induced 2:2 symmetric complex (~7 nm spacing)

Signaling: MyD88, IRAK4, TRAF6, IRF7, IFN-alpha/beta (pDC)

Role in LLPS: End-point sensor; RAGE facilitates CpG-A delivery to TLR9 endosomes

LLPS mechanistic comparison: CpG-A drives HMGB1 phase separation through the RAGE/SLP76 axis (PNAS 2025). The G-quadruplex topology provides multivalent scaffolding that linear ODNs simply cannot replicate. Adenoviral dsDNA triggers a structurally distinct cGAS-DNA LLPS pathway producing cGAMP and activating STING. Adenovirus pre-empts this by directing protein VII to sequester HMGB1 in chromatin through direct A-box contact. CpG-B bypasses both condensate pathways entirely, engaging TLR9 through direct 1:1 endosomal binding and driving NF-kB rather than IRF7 or IRF3. RAGE sits at the intersection, acting as both a DNA receptor and an HMGB1 co-sensor, while TLR4 provides an orthogonal HMGB1 entry point that does not require phase separation.
